## Website Wireframe – Construction & Services AI Automation

This is the layout and section order for your marketing site, mapped to content from `site-content.md`.

---

### 1. Hero Section (Above the Fold)

**Goal:** Grab attention, state the core pain, and push the visitor to book a demo or audit.

**Layout:**
- Left: Headline, subheadline, bullets, CTAs.  
- Right: Image/video mock (dashboard, phone with SMS, or simple illustration of missed call → booked job).

**Content Source (from `site-content.md`):**
- Headline: “Every Missed Call Is a Job Your Competitor Wins”  
- Subheadline: AI‑powered booking & follow‑up system copy.  
- Primary CTA: “Book a 10‑Minute Demo (See how many extra jobs we could be booking for you each month.)”  
- Secondary CTA: “Get a Free ‘Missed Jobs’ Audit (We’ll estimate how much revenue you’re leaving on the table.)”  
- Credibility bar under CTAs.

---

### 2. Credibility / ICP Strip (Just Below Hero)

**Goal:** Quickly signal who this is for so the right people keep reading.

**Layout:**
- Narrow band with text/icons listing industries and ideal client profile.

**Content Source:**
- Industries: general contractors, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, restoration, cleaning.  
- Short line: “Built for construction & service businesses that care about revenue.”

---

### 3. Problem Section

**Goal:** Make them feel the pain of missed calls and slow follow‑up, tied to lost revenue.

**Layout:**
- Left: Problem headline + short paragraphs.  
- Right: Bullet list of symptoms (missed calls, slow quotes, no automation) and a stat callout.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “The Problem (Why You’re Losing 20–40% of Jobs)”.  
- Emphasize: 3–5 companies called, 20–40% jobs slipping away, wasted ad spend.

---

### 4. Solution Overview Section

**Goal:** Present your system as the clear, simple solution.

**Layout:**
- Headline + short paragraph.  
- Grid or checklist of “What’s Included”.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “The Solution (Your Core Offer)”.  
- Items: missed call → SMS, AI qualification, photo‑based analysis, automatic booking, quote follow‑up, review engine.  
- Include one line about “revenue recovery layer” that sits on top of their existing process.

---

### 5. “How It Works” 3‑Step Section

**Goal:** Reduce perceived complexity; show this is a straightforward install.

**Layout:**
- Horizontal 3‑step layout or vertical timeline:
  - Step 1 – Audit & Design  
  - Step 2 – Build & Connect  
  - Step 3 – Launch & Optimize

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Simple 3‑Step Implementation” in `site-content.md`.

---

### 6. Who It’s For / Not For Section

**Goal:** Qualify leads and build trust by being explicit about fit.

**Layout:**
- Two columns:
  - Left: “Who This Is For” with industries + best‑fit profile bullets.  
  - Right: “Who This Is Not For” with 3–4 bullets.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Who This Is For” + “Who This Is Not For”.

---

### 7. Results & Benefits Section

**Goal:** Shift focus to outcomes and financial impact.

**Layout:**
- Headline + benefit bullets.  
- Highlight box with example math scenario.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Results & Benefits”.  
- Include bullets (20–40% more jobs, faster response, higher close rate, more reviews).  
- Add the “Example Scenario” with job value (e.g., extra \$5k/month from 2 recovered jobs).

---

### 8. Proof / Case Study Snapshot Section

**Goal:** Show this working in the real world, even with one simple story.

**Layout:**
- Left: Short case snapshot (industry, problem, result).  
- Right: Quote block from owner (you can add a real quote later).

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Proof & Case Study Snapshot”.  
- Replace placeholder scenario with your first real client once available.

---

### 9. Pricing Section

**Goal:** Be transparent on pricing and reinforce ROI.

**Layout:**
- Single pricing card:
  - Setup fee  
  - Monthly fee  
  - Key inclusions.  
- Under card: short “What the math looks like” paragraph.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Pricing”.  
- Core Package, Launch Incentive, and “What the Math Looks Like” copy.

---

### 10. Guarantee / Risk Reversal Section

**Goal:** Lower risk of taking the first step (demo/signup).

**Layout:**
- Simple block with headline and 2–3 bullet points explaining the guarantee.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “Guarantee & Risk Reversal”.  
- Emphasize 30‑day cancel/“if it doesn’t help you book more jobs, you don’t keep it.”

---

### 11. FAQs Section

**Goal:** Remove remaining objections that block demos.

**Layout:**
- Accordion or list of 5–7 FAQs.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “FAQs” in `site-content.md`.  
- Include:
  - Phone system/CRM  
  - Office staff  
  - Go‑live speed  
  - AI saying something wrong  
  - Compliance  
  - “What do you need from us to start?”

---

### 12. About Section

**Goal:** Humanize you and show you understand contractors and tech.

**Layout:**
- Photo of you (or simple avatar) + short bio text.

**Content Source:**
- Section: “About Us”.  
- Emphasize full‑stack + AI + GHL experience and focus on revenue, not just tools.

---

### 13. Final CTA Section (Footer Band)

**Goal:** Give one last clear next step.

**Layout:**
- Strong headline + single CTA row.

**Content Source:**
- Reuse the “Want to See How Many Jobs You’re Leaving on the Table?” CTA section.  
- Buttons:
  - Primary: “Book a 10‑Minute Demo”  
  - Secondary: “Request a Free ‘Missed Jobs’ Audit”

---

### Implementation Notes

- Keep CTAs consistent across the page: **Demo** and **Missed Jobs Audit**.  
- Add sticky header button: “Book a Demo”.  
- On mobile, ensure hero and CTAs appear **without scrolling**.  
- Once you have real case studies, swap the “Proof” section content and add logos/testimonials near the hero for extra lift.
